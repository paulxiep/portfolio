NOAA Storm Database extreme weather casualties and economic consequences records.

Synopsis

The NOAA extreme weather events record is processed and sorted into 11 disaster categories. Casualties and damages for each category are tallied. Winds, heat waves, and landslides are most hazardous with respect to population health. Floods, tropical storms, and droughts cause the most economic damage.

Introduction

This document explores and analyzes extreme weather events data in the NOAA Storm Database. The data can be downloaded from NOAA Storm Database Link. Further information can be found on NOAA Storm Database website. The data contain records of the occurrence of storms and other significant weather phenomena having sufficient intensity to cause loss of life, injuries, significant property damage, and/or disruption to commerce.

The goal of this report is to answer 2 main questions:

  1. Across the United States, which types of events are most harmful with respect to population health?
  2. Across the United States, which types of events have the greatest economic consequences?

The r code as well as explanation, and justification of each step of data processing and analysis will both be shown and described in this document.

The categories are

  1. wind contains any word that signifies presence of wind, including blizzards and waterspouts.
  2. tropical storm contains tropical storms and hurricanes.
  3. tornado includes, aside from tornados, waterspouts and funnel.
  4. thunder contains anything thunder- or lightning-related.
  5. rain contains a single word rain.
  6. cold contains disasters related to cold weather.
  7. floodortide contains words that signifies floods or abnormally low or hide tides.
  8. fire contains a single word fire.
  9. droughtorheat contains drought, dry spells, and heat waves.
  10. geo contains avalanches and landslides.
  11. water contains any event that occurs on water, be them sea, river, lakes, etc.

However, a description on NOAA site states that before 1996, only tornados, thunderstorms and hails and recorded. This may introduce observational bias into the analysis. It is therefore imparative to cut off the data from before 1996 to eliminate this bias.

dat2 <- subset(dat,DATE>=as.Date("1996-01-01"))

The fatalp, dmgm, fatalp2, and dmgp2 contain the plots of the results.

  1. The first results are the total fatalities, injuries, property and crop damage.
grid.arrange(fatalp, dmgp, nrow = 2)

The wind events cause the most cumulative number of fatalities and injuries. The drought and heat events follows second in fatality count, while tornado comes second in injury count.

In terms of financial damage, flood and tide outstrips wind events despite having a much lower event count. Drought and heat causes the most damage to crops. Financial damage from water- and geo-based events are virtually non-existent, owing to the location and nature of such disaster categories.

  1. The second results shows the fatalities, injuries, property and crop damage per single occurence of each category of events.
grid.arrange(fatalp2, dmgp2, nrow = 2)

Surprisingly, drought and heat events, while seeming less intense than storms or fire, cause the most fatalities and injuries when they do occur, probably because they are more chronic in nature and could last weeks or months. Geo (avalanche and landslide) events have the second-highest fatality rate, but very little injury rate. Tropical storms including hurricanes causes the most injury.

Tropical storm far outstrips other types of events in terms of financial damage per occurence, followed at a distance by drought and heat which seems to (unsurprisingly) only cause crop damage.

Summary

When lives are concerned, spending resources in protection against wind will be most effective, followed by heat waves and droughts. However, high-fatality-intensive events such as landslides and avalanches also merit resources lest one be criticized for incompetency and negligence.

Financially, flood and wind causes the most economic damage, with drought and heat also deserving of attention else one would lose support from the farmers. Tropical storms and hurricanes are the most destructive when they do occur and by the same logic as the above paragraph should be prioritized well.
